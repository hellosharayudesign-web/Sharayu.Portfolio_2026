Government Treasury
Finance Platform

Redesigned a legacy government treasury platform to improve usability, streamline approvals, and enhance compliance workflows for a state-level finance institution.

RoleAssociate UI/UX Designer
CompanyPwC · IFMS Project
Duration4 Months
CopyrightPwC & Its Client
My Contribution

What I worked on.

I worked closely with treasury stakeholders to understand role-based financial workflows and usability gaps in the legacy system. These insights informed wireframe validation and design decisions to improve clarity, efficiency, and compliance.

🎙️
Stakeholder Interviews
Conducted user interviews with treasury officers to surface real workflow pain points.
🔍
Usability Gap Analysis
Identified gaps across approvals, reporting, and payment processes in the legacy system.
✅
Wireframe Validation
Validated system workflows through structured walkthroughs with officers at each stage.
🤝
Cross-functional Collaboration
Worked across teams to define a clear, role-based UI design aligned to compliance needs.
Impact

Results that matter.

99% User-Validated Workflows
Critical treasury flows validated directly with officers before engineering handoff.
Process Simplification
Approvals, payments, and reporting streamlined by removing unnecessary steps.
Senior Usability
Complexity reduced through focused data grouping for non-technical government users.
100% State Scale
Designed for a high-risk, compliance-driven treasury platform at state level.
Delivery Ready
High-fidelity prototypes approved by stakeholders enabling smooth dev handoff.
Audit Confidence
Clearer role separation, traceable approval states, and structured information hierarchy.
Portal Workflows

Defining the system flows.

We re-iterated and refined application workflows in close collaboration with the engineering team, ensuring alignment with regulatory rules and compliance requirements.

Wireframes

From flows to frames.

UI Design

Trust built through visual language.

In the final UI design phase, the platform was crafted to reflect core financial values — trust, security, reliability, and transparency — through a clean, structured visual language aligned with regulatory and banking standards.

Reflection

What I took away.

💡

Learned to design scalable, compliant finance systems by translating complex treasury workflows into intuitive experiences through stakeholder alignment, data-driven decisions, and iterative usability testing.

Banking at Scale

Enterprise MSME banking platform redesign spanning account and statement generation, app login, FD/RD flows, and core banking journeys, optimised for usability, compliance, and scalability across mobile and web.

RoleAssociate UI Designer
CompanyPwC · Private Banking
Duration7 Months
CopyrightPwC & Its Client
My Contribution

What I worked on.

I worked as a UI Designer, leading the Account and Statement Generation stream. I owned UI design execution, visual hierarchy, interaction design, and client-ready prototyping. Worked in collaboration with tech teams on information architecture and workflows, while regularly validating design decisions with my manager and director.

🎨
UI Design Execution
Led visual hierarchy, interaction design, and component-level UI for Account & Statement Generation.
🤝
Cross-functional Collaboration

Prototyping

Problem Space

A gap in MSME banking.

Information Architecture

Wireframes

UI Design & Prototyping

UX Research · Enterprise · POS & Billing

Scaling Enterprise POS
Under Operational Pressure

A multi-format, high-volume restaurant enterprise experiencing operational strain as its legacy POS and billing system failed to scale with growing complexity.

RoleAssociate UX Researcher
TeamExperience Consulting
Duration3 Months
TypeField-led Enterprise Research
My Role

What I worked on.

As an Associate UX Researcher within the Experience Consulting team, I led field research initiatives and collaborated closely with operations and technology stakeholders. I was responsible for designing the research approach, conducting ethnographic inquiry, leading synthesis workshops, and translating behavioural insights into enterprise-aligned recommendations.

👁️
Ethnographic & Contextual Inquiry
Designed the field observation framework and shadowed live billing, payment, and exception-handling workflows.
🏪
User Immersions
Interviewed store managers, frontline staff, and customers across QSR, franchise, airport, and transit outlets.
🔬
Collaborative Synthesis
Led synthesis workshops translating behavioural signals and system gaps into prioritised research findings.
🗺️
Modernisation Roadmap
Contributed to a phased POS modernisation roadmap presentation reviewed by senior leadership.
Problem Space

A system built for yesterday's volume.

A legacy POS system originally designed for lower-volume, predictable workflows was failing to scale with growing operational complexity — creating mounting risk across operations, experience, and revenue integrity.

Key Findings

What we discovered.

During peak periods, manual contingencies added 2–4 minutes per transaction in some outlets — compounding staff load and customer frustration simultaneously.

👷 Employee Experience
No unified, real-time view of sales, orders, and inventory
Paper-based reconciliation increased human error risk
POS downtimes triggered disruptive manual billing workflows
🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Customer Experience
Inconsistent and opaque refund and cancellation experiences
Extended wait times during payment reconciliation lowered trust
Payment uncertainty significantly impacted perceived brand reliability
Reflection

What I took away.

💡

This project reinforced that enterprise experience breakdowns rarely stem from isolated usability issues — they emerge from the interaction between systems, policies, and human behaviour under pressure.

UX Research · EdTech · Moodle LMS

Moodle Hive
AI-Powered Pre-Submission Feedback

A research-led concept exploring how AI-assisted, pre-submission feedback can improve learning outcomes and reduce student uncertainty within large-scale learning management systems.

RoleUX Researcher
CompanyMonash University
Problem

Feedback that arrives too late to matter.

In higher education, feedback is often delivered after grades are released — limiting its ability to support learning. Students report high uncertainty before deadlines, while tutors face increasing pressure to provide meaningful feedback at scale. Within Moodle, feedback tools prioritise submission and grading rather than learning progression.

Design Objective

Support learning before the deadline.

Design a pre-submission feedback experience that supports learning before assessment deadlines, reduces uncertainty without removing productive struggle, scales across units without increasing tutor workload, and integrates seamlessly into existing Moodle workflows.

Key Insights

What the research revealed.

🧠
Inconsistent Feedback Increases Cognitive Load
Students must repeatedly interpret new expectations for each unit, diverting effort away from learning.
🗂️
Learning Artefacts Are Disconnected
Drafts, rubrics, and feedback live in separate spaces, limiting meaningful iteration and reflection.
📅
Planning Tools Support Deadlines, Not Learning
Calendars and checklists track time but don't help students assess quality or readiness.
🙋
Students Want Guidance, Not Answers
Students value clarity, validation, and direction — rather than automated solutions that remove critical thinking.
Reflection

AI as a learning partner.

💡

This project reinforced the importance of designing AI as a learning partner rather than a shortcut. The most effective interventions were those that reduced uncertainty and supported reflection without removing productive struggle from the learning process.

RegionAustralia
Project Overview

End-to-end interiors, brought to print.

Sterling Homes is an Australian home solutions company specialising in end-to-end residential interiors — kitchens, bathrooms, cabinetry, lighting, and storage.
