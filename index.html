Who I am

I'm a Product and UX Designer driven by the belief that design should make complex systems clear, accessible, and meaningful. My interest began with a curiosity about how people make decisions and navigate structured environments, leading me to user experience as a way to connect human behaviour with technology and organisational goals.

Over the past four years, I've worked across finance, government, and enterprise environments, including policy-heavy and approval-based systems. These contexts shaped my ability to understand constraints, analyse workflows, and translate complexity into clarity. At PwC and in other client-facing roles, I collaborated with product managers, developers, and stakeholders to design internal platforms and digital products that support informed decision-making and operational efficiency. Through research, interaction design, and iteration, I focus on creating experiences that build trust as well as usability.

My mission is to bridge complex organisational processes and human understanding through research-led, collaborative design. I aim to create structured, intuitive experiences that help users feel confident and supported.

I'm particularly interested in enterprise and public-sector products where thoughtful design can improve transparency, accessibility, and efficiency at scale. Guided by empathy, curiosity, and continuous learning, I strive to create solutions that are both practical and meaningful.

UX Research · Enterprise · POS & Billing

Scaling Enterprise POS
Under Operational Pressure

A multi-format, high-volume restaurant enterprise was experiencing operational strain as its legacy POS and billing system failed to scale with growing complexity. Originally designed for lower-volume operations, the system struggled under peak-hour demand — leading frontline staff to rely on manual workarounds for billing, refunds, reconciliation, and exception handling.

RoleAssociate UX Researcher
TeamExperience Consulting
Duration3 Months
TypeField-led Enterprise Research
My Role & Responsibilities

What I worked on.

As an Associate UX Researcher within the Experience Consulting team, I led field research initiatives and collaborated closely with operations and technology stakeholders. I was responsible for designing the research approach, conducting ethnographic inquiry, leading synthesis workshops, and translating behavioural insights into enterprise-aligned recommendations.

👁️
Ethnographic & Contextual Inquiry
Designed the field observation framework and shadowed live billing, payment, and exception-handling workflows across formats.
🏪
User Immersions
Interviewed store managers, frontline staff, and customers across QSR, franchise, airport, and transit outlets.
🔬
Collaborative Synthesis
Led synthesis workshops translating behavioural signals and system gaps into clearly prioritised research findings.
🗺️
Modernisation Roadmap
Contributed to a phased POS modernisation roadmap presentation reviewed and validated by senior leadership.
Business Impact

Results that matter.

Operational Clarity
Structural misalignment between system design and live operations identified, guiding leadership prioritisation for the modernisation roadmap.
Manual Reduction Strategy
High-risk billing dependencies surfaced, shaping automation priorities and reducing reliance on human workarounds.
Experience Standardisation
Cross-format inconsistencies exposed, enabling targeted design for scalable, uniform workflows across all outlet types.
Customer Confidence
Transaction clarity gaps identified, informing recommendations to reduce payment uncertainty and rebuild brand trust.
Enterprise Readiness
Research-backed recommendations validated against technical feasibility, governance constraints, data integrity, and multi-format scalability.
Research Coverage

Store formats covered.

🍔 Quick Service (QSR)
🏪 Franchise-Owned (FOFO)
🏢 High-Footfall Company-Owned
✈️ Airport Outlets
🚂 Railway Coach Outlets
Problem Space

A system built for yesterday's volume.

A legacy POS system originally designed for lower-volume, predictable workflows was failing to scale with growing operational complexity — creating mounting risk across operations, experience, and revenue integrity.

⚡Lacked real-time operational visibility under peak demand
💳Payment system failures during transaction surges
📄Manual billing contingencies forced during POS downtime
🔄Inconsistent refund and cancellation workflows across formats
🧠Heavy reliance on staff judgement to compensate for system gaps — increasing cognitive load and operational risk
01
Understand live operational pressure
Observe how POS systems are used under real conditions — peak-hour demand, multi-format complexity, and transaction volume spikes.
02
Identify behavioural workarounds
Document how frontline staff compensate for system failures through improvised manual processes.
03
Map system-to-reality gaps
Evaluate the divergence between intended system design and actual usage across store formats.
04
Assess impact on people
Understand the downstream effect on employee confidence, customer trust, and brand perception.
05
Develop enterprise-aligned recommendations
Translate research into scalable improvement initiatives grounded in technical and governance realities.
Research Methodology

A mixed-method, field-first approach.

Research was conducted under real operating conditions — including high-footfall service hours — with Store Managers, frontline Staff, and Brand Customers as primary participants.

01 — Ethnographic & Observational
Live shadowing of POS, billing, and payment flows in real operational conditions, including system downtime incidents.
02 — Mystery Shopping
End-to-end customer journey assessment across multiple store formats and transaction types.
03 — Structured Interviews
In-depth interviews with store managers and frontline staff to surface workarounds and unmet operational needs.
04 — Rapid Customer Feedback
Short surveys and intercept conversations during peak and off-peak service windows.
Key Findings

What we discovered.

During peak periods, manual contingencies added 2–4 minutes per transaction in some outlets — compounding staff load and customer frustration simultaneously.

👷 Employee Experience
No unified, real-time view of sales, orders, and inventory
Inventory data frequently delayed or maintained offline via paper
Paper-based reconciliation increased human error risk
POS downtimes triggered disruptive manual billing workflows
Payment failures eroded staff confidence and slowed service recovery
🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Customer Experience
No unified customer history accessible across outlets
Inconsistent and opaque refund and cancellation experiences
Extended wait times during payment reconciliation lowered trust
Unclear transaction status during digital payment failures
Payment uncertainty significantly impacted perceived brand reliability
Recommended Priority Initiatives

From insights to action.

Through cross-functional collaboration with operations and technology stakeholders, insights were translated into five prioritised enterprise initiatives — each stress-tested against technical feasibility, governance constraints, data integrity requirements, and multi-format scalability.

01
Role-Based Operational Dashboards
Customised dashboards for managers, inventory teams, and cashiers — providing real-time visibility into sales, stock, and order status to reduce cognitive switching under pressure.
02
Unified System Integration
Seamless integration with online ordering platforms and stable UPI and card payment infrastructure to reduce reconciliation errors and recovery time.
03
Centralised Customer Data
Secure, cross-outlet customer history to enable faster repeat transactions, consistent service, and improved refund resolution across all formats.
04
Digitised QSR Ordering
QR-based table ordering with in-flow digital payments and counter display integration for real-time order tracking — reducing queue pressure and manual handoffs.
05
Standardised Loyalty & Refund Framework
Clear, consistent governance for refunds, cancellations, and loyalty programs — reducing ambiguity for staff and improving customer retention and brand reliability.
Reflection

What I took away.

💡

This project reinforced that enterprise experience breakdowns rarely stem from isolated usability issues — they emerge from the interaction between systems, policies, and human behaviour under pressure. Observing frontline staff compensate for system limitations revealed how cognitive load, ambiguity, and operational risk accumulate during peak service windows.

UX Research · EdTech · Moodle LMS

Moodle Hive
AI-Powered Pre-Submission Feedback

A research-led concept exploring how AI-assisted, pre-submission feedback can improve learning outcomes and reduce student uncertainty within large-scale learning management systems.

RoleUX Researcher
CompanyMonash University
Problem

Feedback that arrives
too late to matter.

In higher education, feedback is often delivered after grades are released — limiting its ability to support learning or improvement. Students report high uncertainty before deadlines, while tutors face increasing pressure to provide meaningful feedback at scale. Within Moodle, feedback tools prioritise submission and grading rather than learning progression, resulting in reactive rather than proactive support.

Why This Matters
Students feel anxious and under-supported before submission
Feedback practices vary significantly across units
Learning artefacts — drafts, rubrics, feedback — are fragmented
Tutors face increasing workload with inconsistent expectations

This creates a systemic gap where feedback exists — but learning impact is minimal.

Design Objective

Support learning before the deadline.

Design a pre-submission feedback experience that supports learning before assessment deadlines, reduces uncertainty without removing productive struggle, scales across units without increasing tutor workload, and integrates seamlessly into existing Moodle workflows.

Key Constraints
Academic integrity Institutional governance Educator oversight preserved
Research Approach

System-level issues,
not isolated usability problems.

A mixed-methods research approach was used to uncover structural gaps in the feedback ecosystem rather than surface-level usability friction.

📚
Literature Review
Feedback timing and learning efficacy in higher education contexts.
🎙️
Semi-Structured Interviews
In-depth conversations with university students about their Moodle experience.
👁️
Observation & Journey Mapping
Mapping student workflows to identify friction points and emotional peaks.
🗂️
Affinity Mapping
Synthesising insights across data sources into actionable themes.
Key Research Questions
Where do students experience the most friction in the Moodle journey?
Which moments create the highest anxiety or confusion?
How can AI support learning without replacing educators?
What interventions are scalable within institutional constraints?
Key Insights

What the research
revealed.

🧠
Inconsistent Feedback Increases Cognitive Load
Students must repeatedly interpret new expectations for each unit, diverting effort away from learning.
🗂️
Learning Artefacts Are Disconnected
Drafts, rubrics, and feedback live in separate spaces, limiting meaningful iteration and reflection.
📅
Planning Tools Support Deadlines, Not Learning
Calendars and checklists track time, but do not help students assess quality or readiness.
🙋
Students Want Guidance, Not Answers
Students value clarity, validation, and direction — rather than automated solutions that remove critical thinking.
Concept Solution

Moodle Hive — an AI learning partner.

Moodle Hive introduces an AI-powered, pre-submission feedback layer embedded directly within Moodle. The system supports students during drafting — helping them assess readiness and alignment before final submission.

Opportunity Areas
✦Rubric-aligned, formative feedback before submission
✦AI-assisted clarity on expectations and structure
✦Real-time learning nudges that support reflection
✦Consistent feedback experiences across units
Key Design Decisions

Guidance over
automation.

🤝
Guidance over automation
AI provides directional feedback rather than generating answers, preserving academic integrity.
⏱️
Pre-submission timing
Feedback delivered before deadlines to maximise learning impact when it matters most.
👩‍🏫
Human-in-the-loop
Tutors retain visibility and control, ensuring institutional trust and adoption.
🔗
System integration
Designed to fit existing Moodle workflows to reduce implementation friction.
Key Features
01
Pre-Submission Feedback
Analyses structure, clarity, and alignment with assessment criteria. Highlights gaps without providing solutions.
02
Learning-Focused Suggestions
Encourages revision, reflection, and self-assessment. Supports critical thinking rather than shortcut behaviour.
03
Submission Readiness Indicator
Helps students gauge preparedness, reducing uncertainty and last-minute anxiety before deadlines.
Prototyping & Validation

Testing for clarity and trust.

Low-fidelity wireframes were used to explore user flows and interaction models. Concept prototypes were tested for clarity, perceived usefulness, and alignment with learning goals. Feedback confirmed the importance of transparency, ethical AI behaviour, and educator oversight.

Impact & Value

Better outcomes for
students and institutions.

🎓 Student Impact
Increased confidence before submission
Improved clarity around expectations
Reduced reliance on external tools
Stronger learning outcomes through iteration
🏛️ Institutional Value
Scalable support without increasing tutor workload
More consistent feedback experiences across units
Reduced reactive feedback cycles post-grading
Improved student satisfaction and retention
Success Metrics (Proposed)
Reduction in last-minute submissions Improved rubric alignment in first submissions Lower student anxiety prior to deadlines Decreased volume of reactive tutor feedback
Reflection

AI as a learning partner.

💡

This project reinforced the importance of designing AI as a learning partner rather than a shortcut. The most effective interventions were those that reduced uncertainty and supported reflection — without removing productive struggle from the learning process. Ethical design, transparency, and human oversight were essential in shaping a solution that enhances trust and long-term learning outcomes.
